JUDGEMENT

B. Sasi Mahendran, J.

The Accused Appellant (hereinafter referred to as the Appellant) was the 10th

witness in the High Court Case No 254/2017.

The Appellant, a reserve police officer, was taken into custody for production on

four occasions. When giving evidence on 1st October 2018, he falsely stated that

he had received the production at 17:50 hours on 8th January 2016 and, at 12:00

noon on the same day, handed it over to Witness Nandani. Later that same day,

he contradicted himself by stating that he had received the production from SI

Manjula. He further testified that on 10th January 2016, he handed over the

production to SI Manjula.

On 4th January 2019, the Appellant was recalled to give evidence. On that

occasion, he explained the reasons for the mistake made on 1st January 2019 and

produced his medical records in support.

In delivering judgment, the Learned High Court Judge accepted the latter version

of events presented by this Appellant, convicted the Accused on 11th October 2019,

and subsequently convicted this Appellant on 16th December 2019 under Section

449 of the Criminal Procedure Code for giving false evidence, imposing a sentence

accordingly.

When the matter came before us on 06.03.2026 for argument, the learned

Additional Solicitor General, who appears for the Attorney General, upholding the

Page 4 of 5

best tradition of the Attorney General's Department, submitted to the court that

he was unable to support the conviction against the Appellant.

I note that when the Appellant gave evidence on 04th January 2019, he informed

the court that due to his poor vision, he was unable to read the brief and give

evidence, but the Learned High Court Judge has failed to consider his version. It

is pertinent to refer to the relevant portion of the Learned High Court Judge’s

judgment.
