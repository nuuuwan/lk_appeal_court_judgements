Judgement

1. The Accused-Appellant (hereinafter referred to as “the Appellant”) was indicted

before the High Court of Monaragala for the following charge;

On or about 23.02.2010, within the jurisdiction of the High Court of

Monaragala, the Appellant, by using the Appellant’s genitals on any orifice

or part of the body of a girl below 16 years, namely, Rathnayake

Mudiyanselage Sachini Nimanthika committed an offence of grave sexual

abuse, which is an offence punishable under Section 365B (2)(b) of the Penal

Code as amended by the Acts No. 22 of 1996, 29 of 1998 and 16 of 2006.

2. Upon the Appellant pleading not guilty to the charge, the matter was then taken up

for trial before the High Court of Monaragala. At the conclusion of the trial, the

Learned High Court Judge found the Appellant guilty of the charge and convicted

him.

3. Accordingly, the Appellant was sentenced to eight years’ rigorous imprisonment. In

addition, the Appellant was ordered to pay a fine of Rs. 20,000/-, in default of which

he is to undergo six months’ rigorous imprisonment. The Appellant was further

directed to pay a sum of Rs. 50,000/- as compensation to the prosecutrix, in default

of which he is to undergo one year’s simple imprisonment.

4. Being aggrieved by the said judgment and the sentence, the Appellant has preferred

the present appeal to this Court.

a. Did the Learned Trial Judge misdirect himself in evaluating the evidence of

f. The Learned High Court Judge has failed to consider the lack of

corroboration and the incredibility of the evidence led by the prosecution.

g. The Learned High Court Judge has failed to consider the improbability and

impossibility of the prosecution's evidence.

h. Did the Learned High Court Judge fail to analyze PW1’s testimony, which

is inter se and per se.

Thereafter, she climbed onto a wooden table situated in the living room together

10. Thereafter, at about 1.30 p.m., the prosecutrix returned home from school,

whereupon, PW2 placed her on a wooden table situated in the living room, changed

her into a gown, asked her to wear an undergarment, and gave her something to eat

and informed PW2 that the Appellant had touched her vaginal area.

11. Upon PW2 proceeding from the kitchen to the living room and questioning the

Appellant regarding the said incident, the Appellant fled from the scene. Then, PW2

immediately proceeded to the Police Station after entrusting the prosecutrix to the

12. It is noteworthy that the evidence of PW2 further corroborated the evidence of the

prosecutrix, and no material infirmities were marked therein that go to the root of

the matter.

13. In addition, PW10, the Chief Investigating Officer, testified that the father of the

prosecutrix came to the Police Station and made a complaint regarding the alleged

14. Upon receiving the complaint, PW10, together with other Police Officers,

proceeded to arrest the Appellant.

15. According to the police observations, the prosecutrix herself showed the wooden

table as the place of the offence. Considering the tender age of the prosecutrix, such

spontaneous identification of the location of the incident lends further assurance to

the truthfulness and reliability of her testimony. This account further strengthens the

credibility of the prosecution’s case.

18. In these circumstances, it is difficult to accept that the father of the prosecutrix

would have gone to the extent of fabricating such serious allegations, merely on

account of a relatively trivial monetary dispute. Accordingly, it is my view that the

nature of the allegation is of such seriousness that it is wholly improbable that it

would have been fabricated for the recovery of a small sum of money.

19. In view of the foregoing evidence, the version of the prosecutrix is corroborated by

clear and cogent evidence adduced through the independent witnesses. Therefore,

the conclusion reached by the Learned Trial Judge that the charge against the

Appellant had been proved beyond a reasonable doubt is legally sustainable.

20. It was contended by the Learned Counsel for the Appellant that the prosecution had

failed to establish the offence of grave sexual abuse as set out in the indictment. The

Learned Counsel further submitted that there exists a material inconsistency in the

prosecution’s evidence. However, in view of the foregoing reasons, there is no merit

in this ground of appeal, as there is clear and cogent evidence adduced through

independent witnesses, which evidence materially corroborated one another and

sufficiently established the charge against the Appellant beyond a reasonable doubt.

21. With regard to the ground of appeal that the Learned High Court Judge failed to

consider the improbability and impossibility of the prosecution’s evidence, it is

important to note that the prosecutrix disclosed the alleged incident to her father

immediately after the occurrence of the alleged act, and the complaint was made on

the very same day without delay. Such conduct demonstrates the spontaneity and

genuineness of the complaint and is wholly consistent with the conduct of

reasonably prudent persons placed in similar circumstances. Therefore, the evidence

adduced by the prosecution cannot be said to be improbable or impossible as

contended by the Appellant.

22. Further, another ground advanced by the Appellant is that the Learned High Court

Judge has failed to properly evaluate the dock statement made by the Appellant.

23. In the dock statement, the Appellant stated that, while he was proceeding towards

the paddy field, the father of the prosecutrix called him to his house to drink illicitly

distilled liquor. The Appellant further stated that PW2 was engaged in the sale of

illicitly distilled liquor. According to the Appellant, after consuming one glass of

the said liquor, he proceeded towards the paddy field. Thereafter, while returning

home, he was again called by PW2 to consume the same.

24. The Appellant further stated that PW2 assaulted him on the allegation that he had

taken a sum of Rs. 700/- from him. Subsequently, according to the Appellant, PW2

assaulted him on the basis that the Appellant struck the back of the prosecutrix when

she was touching an electric wire. The Appellant further stated that, while the father

of the prosecutrix was assaulting him, he fled from the scene. Thereafter, while he

was sleeping at his house, his mother shouted that the police were coming,

whereupon he ran away out of fear that he would be imprisoned for consuming

illicitly distilled liquor.

25. Evidently, the dock statement made by the Appellant is insufficient to create a doubt

in the prosecution’s case, and fails to rebut the clear, cogent, and corroborative

evidence adduced by the prosecution witnesses. Consequently, the said dock

statement does not diminish the probative value of the prosecution’s case.

26. Upon consideration of the foregoing analysis of law and facts, I find no merit in the

arguments advanced on behalf of the defence. Accordingly, there is no basis to

interfere with the judgment of the Learned High Court Judge, and the conviction is

affirmed.

27. Accordingly, the appeal is dismissed.
