K. M. S. DISSANAYAKE, J.

Instant appeal arises from an order dated 13.05.2019, made by the learned High

Court Judge of the Uva Province holden at Monaragala in the exercise of the

revisionary jurisdiction vested in it under Article 154(3)(b) of the Constitution of

the Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka (hereinafter called and referred

to as ‘Constitution’) wherein, the learned High Court Judge had refused to grant

relief to the Respondent-Petitioner-Appellant (hereinafter called and referred to

as ‘the Appellant’) as prayed for in prayer to the petition filed thereat along with

his application in revision on the premise that no miscarriage of justice had

occurred to the Appellant by reason of his Counsel not being afforded an

opportunity to make oral submissions in Court on 20.09.2018.

When this matter came on for hearing before us on 31.10.2025, respective

Counsel for the parties in CA(PHC) 0079/19, CA(PHC) 0081/19, CA(PHC)

0084/19 and CA(PHC) 0087/2019 informed Court that the parties in all the

aforementioned appeals are willing to be bound by the decision that may be

pronounced by this Court in the instant appeal bearing No. CA(PHC) 0082/19.

Hence, judgement of this appeal will bind the parties in all the aforementioned

appeals, namely; CA(PHC) 0079/19, CA(PHC) 0081/19, CA(PHC) 0084/19 and

CA(PHC) 0087/2019 of consent and as urged by them.

The Appellant in the application in revision filed before the High Court of the Uva

Province holden at Monaragala had sought to revise and set aside only the order

of the learned Magistrate of Bibile dated 20.09.2018 (Vide-prayer ‘ආ’ of the

petition). However, it is significant to observe that the Appellant in the instant

appeal seeks in prayer (i) of the petition of appeal to set aside the order of the

learned High Court Judge of Uva Province holden at Monaragala dated

13.05.2019 (hereinafter called and referred to as ‘the order’) while he also seeks

in prayer (iii) of the petition of appeal to set aside the two orders of learned

Magistrate of Bibile dated 19.07.2018 and 20.09.2018. Point to be noted here

is that, the Appellant had not in any manner, sought to revise and set aside the

order of the learned Magistrate of Bibile dated 19.07.2018 in the application in

revision filed before the High Court of the Uva Province holden at Monaragala.

Besides, the Appellant had in the averments in paragraph 1 of his petition filed

before it, specifically, averred that the subject matter of the application in

revision filed before it being the order of the learned Magistrate of Bibile dated

20.09.2018. Hence, the Appellant in prayer (iii) of the petition of appeal filed

before this Court, cannot in law, seek to set aside the order of the learned

Magistrate of Bibile dated 19.07.2018 for; the Appellant had never sought in the

application in revision filed before the High Court of the Uva Province holden at

Monaragala to revise and set aside the order of the learned Magistrate of Bibile

dated 19.07.2018 and therefore, instant appeal insofar as it only, relates to the

prayer (iii) to set aside the order of the learned Magistrate of Bibile dated

19.07.2018 is misconceived both in fact and law and therefore it cannot sustain

in law and as such it should be rejected in-limine.

Let me now, briefly, examine the facts and circumstances relevant to the instant

appeal;

It is in this context, let me examine the nature of the principal relief sought in

prayer ‘ආ’ of his application in revision filed by the Appellant before the High

Court of the Uva Province holden at Monaragala and it reads thus;

“වර් 2018.09.20 වන දින ගරු උගත් මහ ේස්ත්ේරාත්වරයා විසින් නිහයෝග කරන ලද කඩා ඉවත් කිරීහේ නිහයෝගය ක්‍රියාත්මක කිරිම ප්‍රතිහ ෝධනය කරන හලස්ත්ත්.”

Hence, the Appellant had sought to revise a demolition order on the totally,

erroneous assumption that it was one entered by the learned Magistrate of Bibile

on 20.09.2018. However, it is to be observed that the demolition order had in

fact, been made by the learned Magistrate of Bibile on 19.07.2018 and not on

20.09.2018 as erroneously, assumed by the Appellant in the application in

revision and what the learned Magistrate of Bibile did on 20.09.2018 was to order

execution of the demolition order so made by him previously, on 19.07.2018.

Hence, the relief sought by the Appellant in prayer ‘ආ’ of the petition filed in the

High Court of the Uva Province holden at Monaragala along with his application

in revision, was misconceived and therefore, bad in law and as such it being one

that cannot be granted in law. In the circumstances, the instant application in

revision ought to have been dismissed on this ground alone.

On the other hand, as manifest from the minutes of Court dated 17.05.2018, the

Appellant was afforded a reasonable opportunity to present his case on

19.07.2018 on which day Court was informed by the Appellant that he had no

cause to show against the application for demolition order of the unauthorized

construction and therefore, moved Court for time to vacate it and Court had

granted time till 20.09.2018 on which day the learned Magistrate had ordered

execution of the demolition order already entered by him on 19.07.2018 against

the Appellant on the premise that Court had no jurisdiction to set aside the

demolition order so entered by him on 19.07.2018.

Hence, the contention advanced by the Appellant in the High Court of the Uva

Province holden at Monaragala in support of his application in revision that he

was not afforded a reasonable opportunity to present his case in the Magistrate

Court of Bibile, cannot in any manner, sustain both in fact and law in view of

his own documentary evidence so submitted to Court along with his application

in revision marked as ‘X3’ in support thereof, which on the other hand, clearly,

demonstrates that the learned Magistrate of Bibile had afforded him a reasonable

opportunity to present his defence against the application for demolition order

as rightly, observed by the learned High Court Judge of the Uva Province holden

at Monaragala.

I would therefore, hold that the Appellant had not disclosed any exceptional

circumstances warranting the invocation of the extra-ordinary revisionary

jurisdiction vested in the learned High Court Judge of the Uva Province holden

at Monaragala to revise and set aside the order dated 20.09.2018 as rightly, held

by him.

I would therefore, hold that the learned High Court Judge of the Uva Province

holden at Monaragala was entirely, right in refusing to grant to the Appellant

any of the relief so sought in his application in revision by him.

In the result, I would hold that, instant appeal is not entitled to succeed both in

fact and law.

I would thus, dismiss the instant appeal with costs fixed at Rs. 50,000/- to be

payable by the Appellant to the Respondent.

I would affirm the order appealed from.
